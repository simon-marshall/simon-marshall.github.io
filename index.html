CV Research

Hi, I'm a final year PhD student at Universiteit Leiden and I'm interested in transitioning to work in AI alignment.

My PhD dissertation is mostly focused on my contributions to quantum computing, in topics from quantum complexity theory to quantum machine learning and circuit cutting. My personal favourite from this line of research is my paper "untitled", where we provide the strongest conditions currently known that quantum computers can compute functions that classical computers can not.

Outside my PhD I completed a wonderful piece of research with Jan Kirchner using information theory to analyse how models may encode information and how interpretable these encodings would be. I was also involved with the first round of what became METR Evals , assessing the risk posed by modern LLMs.

Before this I was aligned as an undergraduate in Maths+Physics at Warwick University, where I completed my Master dissertation in fluid dynamics under James Sprittles . Through this connection, I can trace my mathematical genealogy back to Poisson, Cauchy, Lagrange, Laplace and Euler.
